6. Reciprocal Rank Fusion

Two rankings, one merged list. RRF combines them by rank, not by score.

How each merged chunk got its score

Why ranks, not scores. A cosine distance near 0.5 and a BM25 score near 3.4 are not on the same scale; adding them directly would let BM25's larger numbers dominate. RRF throws away the magnitudes and keeps only position, so a chunk ranked highly by either retriever gets credit. The constant damps the top-rank advantage so a single first-place finish cannot run away with the merge; 60 is the value from the original RRF paper and it works here without tuning.
